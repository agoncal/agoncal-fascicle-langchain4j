Ella Fitzgerald (1917-1996)
Ella Fitzgerald, dubbed "The First Lady of Song," was one of the most beloved jazz vocalists of all time. Born in Newport News, Virginia, Fitzgerald's pure tone, impeccable diction, and impressive vocal range made her a favorite among audiences and critics. Her collaborations with artists like Louis Armstrong and Duke Ellington, as well as her series of "Songbook" albums, showcased her versatility and mastery of jazz standards. Songs like "Summertime" and "A-Tisket, A-Tasket" remain timeless classics.
