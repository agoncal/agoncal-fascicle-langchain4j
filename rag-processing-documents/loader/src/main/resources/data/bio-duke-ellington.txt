Duke Ellington (1899-1974)
Edward Kennedy "Duke" Ellington was a prolific composer, pianist, and bandleader who elevated jazz to an art form. Born in Washington, D.C., Ellington's career spanned over 50 years, during which he composed more than 1,000 pieces. His orchestra, renowned for its sophisticated sound and rich harmonies, performed classics such as "Mood Indigo" and "It Don't Mean a Thing (If It Ain't Got That Swing)." Ellington's ability to blend jazz, blues, and classical music created a unique and timeless body of work.
