Louis Armstrong (1901-1971)
Louis Armstrong, known as "Satchmo" or "Pops," was a pioneering jazz trumpeter, singer, and bandleader. Born in New Orleans, Armstrong's innovative trumpet style and gravelly voice made him a jazz icon. His charismatic stage presence and groundbreaking recordings like "What a Wonderful World" and "Hello, Dolly!" brought jazz to a global audience. Armstrong's virtuosic playing and improvisational skill set new standards for jazz musicians and influenced countless artists across genres.
